# Visual enrichment plan — The Emotional Rollercoaster of IVF: Why Mental Health Support is Essential

URL: `/blogs/the-emotional-rollercoaster-of-ivf-why-mental-health-support-is-essential`
Category: IVF · Author: Dr. Parth Bavishi · Reviewer: Dr. Himanshu Bavishi

## Rendering notes (applies to all insertion points below)
- `photo` → `externalImage` block. Images downloaded + re-uploaded to Sanity CDN. All photo choices are environment/object shots — no individual faces to avoid implying a real patient.
- `stat-callout` → `statStrip` block (existing, animated).
- **Note on reduced visual count:** This blog is intentionally lighter on structured data — the topic is emotional/qualitative and the text does not contain comparison-table or process-flow data that could be visualised without invention. Four visual points are proposed (within the "4–6" floor) rather than forcing a comparison-table or infographic where no numerical data exists to populate it. All four are tied to specific, named content in the blog.

## Insertion points

### 1. Stat-callout — after "The Impact of IVF on Mental Health"
- **Type:** stat-callout
- **Exact data (from blog text and Key Statistics — all explicitly stated):**
  - ~40% — women undergoing IVF who experience significant emotional distress (direct quote from blog body)
  - 55–65% — IVF success rate under 35 (from Key Statistics)
  - 10–15 — average eggs retrieved per cycle (from Key Statistics)
  - 2–3 weeks — IVF cycle duration (from Key Statistics)
- **Why here:** The 40% stat is the single most impactful number in the entire article. Placing it in a stat-callout immediately below the paragraph that introduces it creates a visual anchor — readers who are skimming will stop here, and it reframes "40% distress" from an alarming figure to a normalized, understood part of the journey that BFI is equipped to address.

### 2. Photo — inside "How Do We Support Our Patients?" (near mindfulness/yoga bullet)
- **Type:** photo
- **Search query:** "yoga mat meditation candles calm room"
- **Why this paragraph:** The support services list explicitly names "Mindfulness and relaxation techniques (meditation, yoga)" as one of four offered services. A clean, calm yoga/meditation setting is a direct visual complement to the named service — not a generic "couple smiling" IVF photo, but a specific reference to what the clinic actually offers.

### 3. Photo — near "The Benefits of Mental Health Support" section
- **Type:** photo
- **Search query:** "open journal pen morning light journaling"
- **Why this paragraph:** The benefits list includes "Increased sense of control over the journey" — journaling is the most widely-recognised practical technique for this exact benefit, and the visual (journal + pen, warm morning light) communicates "personal agency / reflection" without depicting a face or implying a real patient narrative. Keeps the tone introspective rather than clinical.

### 4. Photo — near "Our Promise to Our Patients" / Conclusion
- **Type:** photo
- **Search query:** "two hands clasped together warm light support"
- **Why this paragraph:** The conclusion's core message is "Mental well-being is not separate from fertility care — it is an essential part of it." A hands-clasped image (both hands visible, no face, no identifiable person) is the classic symbolic visual for support and partnership. Cropped at wrist-level it reads as care/comfort without making claims about depicted individuals.

## Blog-listing thumbnail proposal
**Depicts:** A warm-toned flat lay of a small potted plant + an open journal on a wooden desk — calm, hopeful, neutral.
**Why it represents the blog:** The card thumbnail needs to signal "emotional support / mental health" without depicting a distressed or euphoric patient (YMYL risk). A journaling/plant still-life communicates calm, reflective care — appropriate for a blog about the psychological dimension of IVF — and stands visually distinct from the clinical/lab thumbnails used for the other four blogs in this set.
